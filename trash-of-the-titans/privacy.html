Privacy Policy — Crash Reporting and Diagnostic Data

Last updated: 2026-07-13
Applies to: Trash of the Titans ("the Game"), developed by Raccoon Tour ("we," "us," "our").
Contact: tabagaingames@gmail.com

This section describes how the Game collects and processes automated crash reports and diagnostic data. If you have questions about this policy, contact us at the address above.

1. Summary

When the Game crashes or encounters an unhandled error, it may automatically send a diagnostic report to help us find and fix bugs. These reports are designed to contain technical information about the failure, not information about you. We do not use this data for advertising, profiling, or any purpose other than diagnosing and fixing problems in the Game.

2. What we collect

When a crash or error occurs, the Game may transmit the following information to our error-reporting service:

  • Crash details: exception type, error message, stack trace, and the source file and line number where the error occurred.
  • Game state at time of crash: the Game's version and build number, the scene or level that was active, and recent in-game events ("breadcrumbs") such as scene transitions.
  • System information: operating system name and version, CPU and GPU model, amount of system memory, screen resolution, and the language/locale setting.
  • A random installation identifier: a randomly generated identifier that lets us tell whether multiple reports come from the same installation. It is not linked to your Steam account, your real name, your email address, or any other personal identifier we hold.

3. What we do not collect

We have specifically configured our crash reporting to exclude:

  • Your name, email address, Steam ID, or other account identifiers.
  • Your IP address or geographic location beyond what is inherent in the language/locale setting.
  • The contents of your save files, screenshots, chat messages, or any user-generated content.
  • Any device fingerprint or hardware serial numbers.
  • Any data from other applications on your system.

4. Legal basis for processing (EEA / UK users)

For users in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, and other jurisdictions with similar laws, we process crash-report data on the basis of our legitimate interest (Article 6(1)(f) GDPR) in maintaining a stable, functioning product and diagnosing defects. We have determined that this processing is necessary for that purpose, is limited to the minimum data required, and does not override your rights and freedoms given the anonymized nature of the data collected.

You may object to this processing at any time by disabling crash reporting in the Game's settings menu.

5. How the data is used

Crash reports are used solely to:

  • Identify, reproduce, and fix bugs and stability problems in the Game.
  • Prioritize fixes based on how many players are affected by a given issue.
  • Verify that a fix in a subsequent update has resolved the issue.

We do not sell, rent, or share crash-report data with third parties for their own purposes, and we do not use it for advertising, profiling, or any automated decision-making that affects you.

6. Third-party service providers

We use the following service to receive and store crash reports on our behalf:

  • Sentry (Functional Software, Inc., operating as Sentry) — a self-hosted or hosted error-monitoring service. Sentry acts as a data processor and processes crash-report data only on our instructions. Sentry's own privacy documentation is available at https://sentry.io/privacy/.

Sentry's servers are located in the United States. Where data is transferred outside your jurisdiction, we and Sentry rely on Standard Contractual Clauses or equivalent safeguards.

7. Data retention

Crash reports are retained for 30 days after they are received, after which they are automatically deleted. Aggregated statistics derived from crash reports (for example, "X% of players on version 1.2 hit crash Y") may be retained indefinitely, as this aggregated data cannot be used to identify any individual installation.

8. Your rights

Depending on where you live, you may have the following rights with respect to your personal data:

  • The right to know what data we hold about you and to request a copy.
  • The right to request correction or deletion of that data.
  • The right to object to or restrict our processing of it.
  • The right to lodge a complaint with a data-protection authority.

Because crash reports are collected without account identifiers, we may not be able to link a specific report back to you. If you can identify a report (for example, by providing the installation identifier shown in the Game's settings, or by telling us the approximate date and platform), we will do our best to honor your request. Contact us at tabagaingames@gmail.com to exercise any of these rights.

9. Your choices

You can disable crash reporting at any time in the Game's Settings menu. When disabled, no crash reports will be transmitted, though the Game may still write local log files on your own device to help you diagnose problems.

10. Children's privacy

The Game is not directed to children under the age of 13 (or the equivalent minimum age in your jurisdiction — 16 in parts of the EU). We do not knowingly collect data from children. Because crash reports do not include account or identity information, we cannot identify whether a report came from a child. If you believe a child has used the Game and you want any associated data deleted, contact us at tabagaingames@gmail.com and we will take reasonable steps to remove it.

11. California residents (CCPA / CPRA)

If you are a California resident, you have the right to know what personal information we collect about you, to request its deletion, and to opt out of any "sale" or "sharing" of personal information. We do not sell or share crash-report data. The categories of personal information described in Section 2 above are collected for the business purpose described in Section 5.

12. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be announced in the Game's patch notes or on our Steam Community Hub. The "Last updated" date at the top of this policy reflects the most recent revision.

This document was prepared as a good-faith description of our data practices. It is not a substitute for legal advice. If you have questions about how it applies to you, please contact us.
